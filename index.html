How To Play

The rules are very simple:

  • Rock beats Scissors, but loses to Paper.
  • Scissors beats Paper, but loses to Rock.
  • Paper beats Rock, but loses to Scissors.

    Fun Facts of RPS game

  • A Japanese version of the game, called Jan-Ken-Pon, uses the same hand postures but they represent a village chief, his mother, and a tiger.
  • The World Rock Paper Scissors Society holds annual Championship competitions for prizes of up to $10,000.
  • The game has been known by many other names, such as: Rochambeau, ick-ack-ock, ching-chang-walla, piedra-papel-tijera, and schnick-schnack-schnuck!
